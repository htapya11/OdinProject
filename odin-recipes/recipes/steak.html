Steak Recipe

Description

A classic pan-seared steak, seasoned with salt and pepper, cooked to your desired doneness in a hot skillet with olive oil and butter, and optionally flavored with garlic and fresh herbs.

Ingredients

  • 2 ribeye or New York strip steaks, about 1 inch thick
  • Salt and pepper
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons butter

Steps

  1. Take the steaks out of the refrigerator about 30 minutes before cooking to bring them to room temperature.
  2. Pat the steaks dry with paper towels and season generously with salt and pepper on both sides.
  3. Heat a heavy skillet (preferably cast iron) over medium-high heat until very hot, about 5 minutes.
  4. Flip the steaks and add the butter, garlic cloves, and thyme or rosemary to the skillet. Tilt the skillet slightly and use a spoon to continually baste the steaks with the melted butter for another 4-5 minutes, or until the steak reaches your desired doneness.
  5. Remove the steaks from the skillet and let them rest on a cutting board or plate for 5 minutes before serving. This allows the juices to redistribute.
  6. Serve the steaks whole or sliced, drizzled with any remaining butter from the skillet.
